Briarda

by ivy Duckett

T he briard, or bergre de brie, is a large breed of dog tradiotionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by thr French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the first World War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of briard is slowly recovering, Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
